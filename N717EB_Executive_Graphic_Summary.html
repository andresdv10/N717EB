Executive Summary

The plan was revised to align the total workload with a realistic manpower baseline: four technicians, eight productive hours per day, and approximately 45 working days.

Planning Basis

Traxxall Due List Work Card Package Status Report Logbook Review Maintenance Manual

Main Objective

Complete overdue maintenance tasks, execute the full work package structure, address inspection findings, perform functional checks, and configure the aircraft after maintenance completion.

Planning Position

The schedule assumes no major hidden damage, no automatic full hydraulic hose replacement, and engine scope controlled by records, applicability and condition findings.

Daily productive capacity

A 20–25 day schedule would require approximately 9 technicians on average. The revised 45-day plan is therefore the realistic baseline for a four-technician team.

Main Work Drivers

The combined A+B inspection remains the largest driver. Fuel, operational checks, opening, landing gear, structures and engine work define the critical planning path.

Key Assumptions

Hydraulic Hoses

Landing gear and brake hydraulic hoses are treated as condition-inspection items. Full scheduled replacement is not part of the baseline unless findings require corrective action.

Engine Package

LH engine records must be reconciled against Traxxall. The baseline does not assume internal engine inspection unless the records or task status require it.

Schedule

The planning window assumes four base technicians, 32 productive MH/day, material availability and no major hidden structural or fuel tank findings.
